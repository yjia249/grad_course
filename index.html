Graduate Selective Course

Introduction to Soft Matter and Biophysics

Research-paper-based teaching website

16 classes (90 minutes each): 1 opening class and 15 paper-centered topic lectures. Students can use this site during class and for post-class review.

Teaching Design

  • Start from one research paper per topic.
  • Explain what to calculate first and why.
  • Teach interpretation logic, not only final conclusions.
  • Build continuity from method to figure to physical message.

How to Use During Class

  1. Open the topic page from the lecture list below.
  2. Follow the 90-minute timeline section by section.
  3. Switch to the paper PDF for each figure discussion.
  4. Return to discussion prompts at the end.

Recommended Reading (Expandable)

Add papers here any time during the semester. Keep 1-2 sentence notes so students know why each one is worth reading.

Scalliet, Guiselin, Berthier (2022), Thirty Milliseconds in the Life of a Supercooled Liquid, PRX 12, 041028. Note: Deep-supercooling relaxation pathway and facilitation-relevant dynamics.
Tighe, Woldhuis, Remmers, van Saarloos, van Hecke (2010), Model for the Scaling of Stresses and Fluctuations in Flows near Jamming, PRL 105, 088303. Note: Scaling theory for stress and velocity fluctuations near point J, including four rheological regimes.
McMullen, Munoz Basagoiti, Zeravcic, Brujic (2022), Self-assembly of emulsion droplets through programmable folding, Nature 610, 502-506. Note: Programmable droplet chains that fold into designed target structures through pathway control.
Behera, Rao, Sastry, Vaikuntanathan (2023), Enhanced Associative Memory, Classification, and Learning with Active Dynamics, PRX 13, 041043. Note: Nonequilibrium active dynamics enhances memory and learning performance.
Gu, Guiselin, Bain, Zuriguel, Bartolo (2025), Emergence of collective oscillations in massive human crowds, Nature 638, 112-119. Note: Active-matter theory applied to real crowd dynamics and safety-relevant oscillations.
Caprini, Cecconi, Maggi, Marini Bettolo Marconi (2020), Activity-controlled clogging and unclogging of microchannels, Phys. Rev. Research 2, 043359. Note: Active particles make reversible plugs in microchannels, giving a clean entry point into clogging physics.
Paulsen, Keim (2025), Mechanical Memories in Solids, from Disorder to Design, Annual Review of Condensed Matter Physics 16, 61-81. Note: A clear review of how solids remember deformation history, linking amorphous matter, hysteresis, and mechanical metamaterials.
Scalliet, Berthier (2019), Rejuvenation and Memory Effects in a Structural Glass, Physical Review Letters 122, 255502. Note: A sharp lecture-ready paper linking structural-glass aging to rejuvenation and memory under temperature cycling.
Bi, Yang, Marchetti, Manning (2016), Motility-Driven Glass and Jamming Transitions in Biological Tissues, PRX 6, 021011. Note: A strong interdisciplinary phase-transition paper connecting tissue mechanics, cell motility, and jamming.
Ozawa, Berthier, Biroli, Rosso, Tarjus (2018), Random critical point separates brittle and ductile yielding transitions in amorphous materials, PNAS 115, 6656-6661. Note: A sharp yielding paper showing how preparation history controls brittle versus ductile response.
Stern, Arinze, Perez, Palmer, Murugan (2020), Supervised learning through physical changes in a mechanical system, PNAS 117, 14843-14850. Note: A clear physical-learning paper where a mechanical sheet is trained on examples and evaluated on generalization.
